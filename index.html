Durga Puja

Celebrating the Triumph of Goodness and Life

Honoring Goddess Durga's Victory Over Evil and the Joy of Creation

Durga Puja, also known as Durgotsava or Shaaradotsava, is a beloved annual festival from the Indian subcontinent that honors the Hindu goddess Durga. It commemorates her victory over the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. Beyond its religious significance, the festival also celebrates Durga as the motherly power behind life and creation, marking it as a harvest festival as well. This year, Durga Puja will take place from October 9th to October 13th, 2024, filling the air with joy, devotion, and a sense of renewal as communities unite to worship and celebrate.

Diwali

The Festival of Lights and New Beginnings

Embracing the Spiritual Victory of Light Over Darkness and the Power of Knowledge

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, symbolizes the spiritual triumph of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance. Celebrated with great enthusiasm, it marks a time when families and communities come together to light oil lamps, decorate their homes, and embrace the joy of new beginnings. Diwali typically falls between mid-September and mid-November, during the Hindu lunisolar months of Ashvin and Kartika. Beyond the festivities, it is a moment for reflection, renewal, and hope, as the flickering lights remind us of the power of positivity and the promise of a brighter tomorrow.

Date: Mon, 28 Oct, 2024 ♦ Sun, 3 Nov, 2024
